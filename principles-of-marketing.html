Course

Principles of Marketing

Foundational marketing strategy, customer value creation, and real-world case-based problem solving.

Overview

Course Description

This course introduces students to the core principles of marketing through a hands-on and collaborative approach. Students learn how marketing creates and exchanges value across products, services, ideas, and people while building a strong foundation in key marketing concepts, including the six Ps of marketing—product, price, place, promotion, people, and process.

The course emphasizes the practical application of marketing theory. Through real-world cases, students approach business problems from a marketing perspective and develop solutions that connect marketing concepts to organizational decision-making.

Goals

Course Objectives

  • Understand the basic principles and strategic role of marketing
  • Explain how firms create, communicate, and deliver customer value
  • Approach real-world business problems from a marketing perspective
  • Apply core concepts such as segmentation, targeting, positioning, and the marketing mix
  • Understand the role of market research and empirical observation in marketing decisions
  • Work productively in teams and communicate marketing ideas effectively
  • Strengthen written communication through applied marketing assignments
  • Recognize the importance of ethics and inclusivity in marketing practice
Topics

What Students Learn

Customer Value and Exchange

Students learn how marketing creates value for customers and organizations, and how exchange processes shape marketplace relationships.

Segmentation, Targeting, and Positioning

Students learn how firms identify attractive customer segments, choose target markets, and position offerings effectively in competitive environments.

Buyer Behavior Process

Students examine the consumer decision-making process—from need recognition and information search to evaluation and purchase—and how marketers use these insights to design more effective strategies.

The Marketing Mix

Students develop an understanding of product, price, place, and promotion decisions and how these elements work together to support marketing strategy.

Marketing Analytics and Research

Students learn how data, analytics, and research methods are used to understand customer behavior and inform evidence-based marketing decisions.

Digital and Modern Marketing

Students examine how digital channels, platforms, and technologies shape modern marketing, and how firms use contemporary marketing methods to engage customers across the digital customer journey.

Case-Based Problem Solving

Students act as marketing consultants and respond to real business cases, applying course concepts to practical managerial challenges.

Team Collaboration

Students work in teams to analyze business problems, develop solutions, and communicate their recommendations clearly and professionally.

Ethics and Inclusivity in Marketing

Students consider the ethical and inclusive dimensions of marketing decisions and evaluate how firms can create value responsibly.

Pedagogy

Applied Learning

A major component of the course is the use of real-world marketing case studies. Each week, students engage with an actual business problem and apply marketing concepts to generate practical solutions. This structure helps students connect theory to realistic marketplace situations.

The course also includes quizzes, written work, case analysis, team collaboration, and exams, allowing students to demonstrate both conceptual understanding and applied problem-solving skills throughout the semester.
