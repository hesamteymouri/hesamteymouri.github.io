Publications & Projects

Research

My research examines customer-focused marketing strategies using behavioral data to generate actionable managerial insights. I study how firms can better understand customer experiences, evaluations, and decision processes to design marketing practices that create value for both organizations and customers. A primary focus of my research is how customers experience and respond to technologies that take on roles traditionally performed by human employees, including AI-driven decision systems and technology-mediated service interactions.

Courses

Teaching

My teaching emphasizes experiential learning and real-world applications of marketing concepts through data analysis, digital tools, and case-based discussion. I aim to create courses that are forward-looking, practical, and accessible to students from diverse backgrounds.

My teaching philosphy is built on three core principles: applied, industry-relevant learning; future-looking pedagogy shaped by research and technological change; and student-centered instruction that promotes access, confidence, and success for diverse learners.
