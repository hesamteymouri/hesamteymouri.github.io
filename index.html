About Me

Dr. Hesam Teymouri Athar

I teach at the University of New Haven, Pompea College of Business. I received my Ph.D. in Marketing from Oklahoma State University.

My research examines consumer-based strategies in technology-enabled service and digital environments, with a focus on service automation and consumer–technology interactions, exploring transformative service research.

My work combines strong theoretical foundations with rigorous empirical methods to generate insights with clear managerial relevance.

Publications & Projects

Research

My research examines customer-focused marketing strategies using behavioral data to generate actionable managerial insights. A primary focus is how customers experience and respond to technologies that take on roles traditionally performed by human employees, including AI-driven decision systems and technology-mediated service interactions.

Published

Political Ideology, Customer Entitlement, and Complaining Behavior

Exploring how political ideology influences consumers' expectations of service and their tendency to voice complaints when those expectations are unmet.

Consumer Entitlement and Responses to Self-Service Technologies

Exploring how customer entitlement influences reactions to self-service technologies and why some consumers resist taking on tasks traditionally performed by service employees.

Retail Donation Design and Prosocial Behavior

Examining how retail donation design influences charitable giving, engagement, and related consumer responses — showing how marketplace design can encourage prosocial action.

In Progress

Agentic AI and Consumer Reactions to Automated Decisions

Investigating how agentic AI systems that replace human judgment in service decisions affect consumers' feelings of control and autonomy, and how these perceptions influence marketplace responses.

Target: Journal of Marketing · FT50 · ABDC A*

In Progress

Who is Artificial Intelligence? A BERTopic-validated Systematic Review and Role Construal Framework

Introduces the AI Role Construal (ARC) framework, proposing that consumers infer distinct social roles from AI systems (partner, supervisor, or judge), each shaping customer responses differently. Validated through a PRISMA systematic literature review of 1,307 papers and BERTopic topic modeling, the framework identifies structural gaps in how AI roles have been studied across consumer contexts.

Target: Journal of the Academy of Marketing Science · FT50 · ABDC A*

In Progress

Digital Tipping Prompts and Consumer Generosity

Examines how the timing of digital tip prompts influences consumer generosity. Drawing on construal-level theory, this research shows that pre-service and post-service prompts activate different mindsets, shaping how consumers evaluate the tipping decision.

Target: Journal of Service Research · ABDC A*

In Progress

Brand Transgression and Customer Moral Grandstanding

Studying how consumers respond to brand misconduct and how moral signaling influences public criticism and social media backlash.

Target: Journal of Consumer Psychology · FT50 · ABDC A*
