Research

My research examines how emerging technologies and service design shape customer experiences, judgments, and behaviors. I focus on questions at the intersection of consumer behavior, technology, and marketplace well-being.

Research Project

Agentic AI in the Service Frontline

I study how AI-based service decisions shape perceptions of control, fairness, and satisfaction, focusing on consumer responses when important marketplace outcomes are determined by nonhuman agents.

Research Project

Self-Service Technologies

This work investigates why some customers resist self-service technologies, particularly when these systems are perceived as effortful or misaligned with customer expectations.

Research Project

Retail Donation Design and Prosocial Behavior

I examine how retail donation design influences charitable giving, engagement, and related consumer responses, showing how marketplace design can encourage prosocial action.

Research Project

Brand Transgression and Customer Moral Grandstanding

Studying how consumers respond to brand misconduct and how moral signaling influences public criticism and social media backlash.
