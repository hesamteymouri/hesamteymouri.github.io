Methods

Behavioral experiments, Regression-Based Modeling, Difference-in-Difference, Mixed-Effects Modeling.

Research

My research focuses on consumer–technology interactions, retail and service experience design, and customer dissatisfaction and well-being.

Agentic AI in the Service Frontline

Examining how AI-based service decisions influence consumer perceptions of control, fairness, and satisfaction.

Self-Service Technologies

Investigating why certain customers resist self-service technologies and how entitlement affects adoption.

Prosocial Consumer Behavior

Studying how retail donation design influences charitable giving and consumer engagement.

Teaching

My teaching emphasizes experiential learning and real-world applications of marketing concepts through data analysis, digital tools, and case studies. My classes are designed to be forward-looking and accessible to students from a wide range of backgrounds.
