Course

Digital Marketing

Strategy, customer journey design, and analytics in modern digital environments.

Overview

Course Description

This course introduces students to digital marketing as a strategic discipline. Students learn how organizations build digital presence, attract customers, and create value across multiple online touchpoints.

The course emphasizes practical marketing decision-making and explores how digital tools support customer acquisition, engagement, and retention.

Objectives

Course Objectives

  • Understand the strategic role of digital marketing in modern organizations
  • Analyze the digital customer journey across multiple platforms
  • Evaluate website performance and digital presence
  • Understand SEO, SEM, social media marketing, and email marketing
  • Use digital analytics to support marketing decisions
  • Develop an integrated digital marketing strategy
Topics

What Students Learn

AI-Powered Market Research

Students use AI tools to collect and analyze customer reviews, social media posts, and online forums to extract insights, identify trends, and build customer personas that inform marketing strategy.

Website Strategy & Design

Students learn how to build and optimize a website from scratch, structure content for conversion, and evaluate landing page performance against marketing objectives.

Search Engine Marketing (SEM)

Students learn how paid search works, including keyword research, ad copywriting, bidding strategies, and performance evaluation. Topics include PPC ads, display ads, and YouTube advertising, with hands-on experience using Google Ads.

Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

Students learn on-page and off-page SEO techniques, covering keyword strategy, metadata optimization, backlink analysis, and improving organic search rankings using tools like Google Search Console and Semrush.

Social Media Marketing

Students learn how to design paid social campaigns, build audiences, test creative formats, and evaluate performance metrics. Topics include organic social strategy, paid campaigns, A/B testing ad creative, and interpreting results, with hands-on experience using Meta Ads Manager across Facebook and Instagram.

Email Marketing & Automation

Students design email campaigns and automated customer journeys using Mailchimp, covering list segmentation, subject line testing, and drip sequences. The course also covers the legal framework governing email marketing, including CAN-SPAM, GDPR, and opt-in best practices.

Web Analytics

Students use Google Analytics 4 to analyze website traffic, track user behavior, set up conversion events, and build marketing funnels — translating data into actionable insights for campaign optimization.

AI Chatbots & Customer Experience

Students design and deploy conversational marketing experiences using chatbot platforms such as Landbot, building automated customer journeys that handle inquiries, qualify leads, and guide users through the sales funnel.

Pedagogy

Applied Learning Approach

Students collaborate in teams on hands-on projects that reflect real digital marketing practice. They use modern analytics platforms, digital marketing tools, and AI-assisted analysis to evaluate website performance, generate customer insights, and design integrated marketing campaigns. Through these assignments, students learn to connect digital data, strategy, and creative decision making in realistic marketing environments.

Students also work with real digital analytics data to answer marketing questions and generate strategic insights.
