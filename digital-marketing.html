Digital Marketing

Strategy, analytics, and customer journey design in modern digital environments.

Course Description

This course introduces students to digital marketing as a strategic discipline. Students learn how organizations build digital presence, attract customers, and create value across multiple online touchpoints.

The course emphasizes practical marketing decision-making and explores how digital tools support customer acquisition, engagement, and retention.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the strategic role of digital marketing in modern organizations
  • Analyze the digital customer journey across multiple platforms
  • Evaluate website performance and digital presence
  • Understand SEO, SEM, social media marketing, and email marketing
  • Use digital analytics to support marketing decisions
  • Develop an integrated digital marketing strategy

What Students Learn

Market Research Using AI

Students collect text data from online sources such as reviews, forums, and social media and use AI tools to analyze it. The goal is to identify customer insights, trends, and perceptions that can inform marketing decisions.

Website Strategy

Students learn how websites function, how to build a basic website from scratch, and how websites support digital marketing through content, structure, and customer conversion.

Search Engine Marketing

Students learn how to design and run search engine marketing campaigns, including Google PPC and display advertising, and how to evaluate their effectiveness in reaching target audiences and driving traffic.

Search Engine Optimization

Students learn how search engines work and how websites can improve their ranking on search engine results pages (SERPs) to attract more visitors.

Social Media Marketing

Students learn how to use storytelling and design social media advertising campaigns to engage audiences, build brand awareness, and promote products or services.

Email Marketing

Students learn how to design email marketing campaigns and automate customer communication while following legal and ethical guidelines for email marketing.

Web Analytics

Students learn how to use Google Analytics 4 to analyze website traffic, extract customer insights, and build marketing funnels to evaluate and improve digital marketing performance.

Applied Learning

Students work on practical assignments that connect digital strategy to real marketing decisions. Projects involve analyzing website performance, designing digital campaigns, and developing integrated digital marketing strategies.

Students also work with real digital analytics data to answer marketing questions and generate strategic insights.
