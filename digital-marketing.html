Digital Marketing

Course Overview

Course Description

This course introduces students to digital marketing as a strategic, customer-focused discipline. It explores how organizations build and strengthen their digital presence, create value across online touchpoints, and respond to rapidly changing market conditions.

Students develop practical knowledge of major digital marketing tools and tactics while learning how those tools fit into a broader marketing strategy. The course is designed to help students think critically about how firms attract, convert, and retain customers in digital environments.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the strategic role of digital marketing in contemporary organizations.
  • Learn how different digital channels work together across the customer journey.
  • Develop the ability to evaluate and improve a website’s visibility, content, and conversion potential.
  • Build familiarity with SEO, SEM, social media, email marketing, and web analytics.
  • Use data and key performance indicators to support digital marketing decisions.
  • Create and present an integrated digital marketing strategy grounded in research and clear objectives.

What Students Learn

Digital Market Research

Use online tools to generate insights about customers, markets, and opportunities.

SEO and Search Strategy

Understand how search engines work and how websites can improve organic visibility and performance.

Search Engine Marketing

Learn paid search principles, campaign design, and ways to evaluate and improve conversion outcomes.

Website Marketing

Explore website structure, landing pages, and how web design supports business goals and customer experience.

Social Media Marketing

Design social media campaigns and think strategically about content, engagement, and platform choice.

Email Marketing

Apply best practices for communicating with current and prospective customers through email.

Web Analytics

Identify meaningful KPIs and evaluate digital performance across the customer journey and conversion funnel.

Digital Footprint Evaluation

Assess the overall digital presence of an organization or individual and recommend improvements.

Applied Learning

The course emphasizes hands-on and team-based learning. Students work on practical assignments that connect digital strategy to real marketing decisions, moving from market research and website planning to content, advertising, analytics, and customer journey design.

A major applied component involves working with website analytics data from the Google Merchandise Store to answer business questions using real-world-style digital data. Students also develop a strategic digital marketing plan that integrates value proposition, segmentation, targeting, objectives, tactics, metrics, and a customer journey map.

Professional Value

This course is designed to help students build skills that are directly relevant to digital marketing roles. By the end of the course, students are expected to be better prepared to discuss digital strategy, campaign design, analytics, and customer journey planning in professional settings.

The course also encourages students to strengthen their technical and career-ready skill set through optional certifications in areas such as digital marketing, SEO, coding fundamentals, and inbound marketing.

Learning Environment

The course is discussion-based and participation-oriented. Students are expected to contribute thoughtfully, collaborate effectively with peers, and engage with practical marketing problems in a respectful and professional manner.
