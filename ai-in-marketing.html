Course

AI in Marketing

Hands-on application of artificial intelligence to content creation, customer insights, personalization, and AI-supported marketing strategy.

Overview

Course Description

This course prepares students to use artificial intelligence (AI) in modern marketing practice. As companies increasingly rely on AI tools to generate content, analyze customer data, personalize experiences, and automate decisions, there is growing demand for marketers who can effectively work with and manage AI systems.

The course takes a hands-on, applied approach to AI in marketing. Students learn how to use AI tools to design marketing campaigns, generate and evaluate content, analyze customer insights, and build AI-assisted customer experiences. Rather than focusing on technical programming, the course emphasizes practical skills, strategic thinking, and critical evaluation of AI outputs.

Throughout the semester, students work on real-world marketing tasks using AI tools and develop a semester-long project where they design an AI-powered marketing strategy for a business.

Objectives

Course Learning Objectives

  • Use AI tools to generate and refine marketing content (ads, emails, social media)
  • Apply prompt design techniques to improve AI-generated outputs
  • Analyze customer insights using AI-assisted methods
  • Evaluate AI-generated marketing content in terms of effectiveness, clarity, and trust
  • Design AI-supported marketing strategies across the customer journey
  • Develop and present a complete AI-driven marketing plan
  • Critically assess the strengths and limitations of AI in marketing decision-making
Topics

What Students Learn

AI and Consumer Behavior

Students evaluate AI versus human outputs and examine persuasion, trust, and control in AI-supported marketing decisions.

Prompt Design as a Marketing Skill

Students learn how to design, test, and refine prompts to improve the quality and usefulness of AI-generated outputs.

AI for Market Research and Customer Insights

Students analyze customer reviews and text data using AI to identify themes, needs, pain points, and actionable insights.

AI-Driven Content Creation and Brand Voice

Students develop brand-aligned content using AI for campaigns, messaging, and platform-specific communication.

Social Media Strategy and Content Planning

Students build AI-supported content plans and platform-specific strategies for social media marketing.

AI-Enhanced Advertising and Campaign Design

Students generate, compare, and refine ad variations and campaign concepts using AI tools.

Visual Marketing and Creative Generation

Students create and evaluate AI-supported visuals and creative assets for marketing campaigns.

Customer Experience Design and AI Chatbots

Students design chatbot interactions and AI-supported customer journeys across marketing touchpoints.

Personalization and Recommendation Strategy

Students develop AI-supported personalization strategies that tailor messages and experiences to customer needs.

AI-Driven Analytics and Optimization

Students interpret AI-generated insights and design testing and optimization plans to improve campaign performance.

Pedagogy

Applied Learning Approach

This course is highly hands-on and application-focused. Each class session includes short conceptual lectures, live demonstrations of AI tools, in-class exercises, and group-based activities simulating real marketing tasks.

Students regularly create, test, and refine marketing outputs using AI tools. A semester-long group project allows students to apply these skills in a realistic business context.

Assessment

Course Requirements & Assessment

Students complete individual assignments, AI-based examinations, participation-based in-class work, and a semester-long group project. Individual assignments focus on prompting, customer insight extraction, evaluation of AI-generated content, and output improvement.

The group project is completed through multiple milestones across the semester, including customer segmentation, content development, customer experience design, and testing strategy.

  • Midterm Exam (Individual): 200 points
  • Final Exam (Individual): 200 points
  • Individual Assignments (4 total): 200 points
  • Group Project Milestones (4 total): 200 points
  • Final Group Project (Report + Presentation): 150 points
  • Participation & In-Class Activities: 50 points
Expectations

Student Expectations

  • Bring a laptop to class for live AI use and in-class exercises
  • Actively engage in hands-on, application-based learning
  • Be willing to experiment with new AI tools used in marketing contexts
  • Think critically about AI-generated outputs rather than relying on them blindly
  • Work cooperatively in team-based projects and activities
  • Clearly explain and justify how AI was used in your work
