AI in Marketing

Hands-on, applied use of artificial intelligence tools for modern marketing strategy, content, and customer experience design.

University of New Haven
Pompea College of Business
Overview

Course Description

This course prepares students to use artificial intelligence (AI) in modern marketing practice. As companies increasingly rely on AI tools to generate content, analyze customer data, personalize experiences, and automate decisions, there is growing demand for marketers who can effectively work with and manage AI systems.

The course takes a hands-on, applied approach to AI in marketing. Students will learn how to use AI tools to design marketing campaigns, generate and evaluate content, analyze customer insights, generate and validate synthetic consumer data, and build AI-assisted customer experiences. Rather than focusing on technical programming, the course emphasizes practical skills, strategic thinking, and critical evaluation of AI outputs.

Throughout the semester, students will work on real-world marketing tasks using AI tools and develop a semester-long project where they design an AI-powered marketing strategy for a business. The course is designed to reflect how AI is currently used in industry and to prepare students for roles where the ability to effectively use AI is a key competitive advantage.

Objectives

Course Objectives

✓Use AI tools to generate and refine marketing content (ads, emails, social media)
✓Apply prompt design techniques to improve AI-generated outputs
✓Analyze customer insights using AI-assisted methods
✓Evaluate AI-generated marketing content in terms of effectiveness, clarity, and trust
✓Design AI-supported marketing strategies across the customer journey
✓Develop and present a complete AI-driven marketing plan
✓Critically assess the strengths and limitations of AI in marketing decision-making
Topics

What Students Learn

AI & Consumer Behavior

Students evaluate how AI-generated content compares to human-created outputs in terms of persuasion, trust, and consumer response.

Prompting as a Marketing Skill

Students learn prompt design, iteration, and output improvement — treating prompting as a core strategic marketing competency.

AI for Market Research

Students analyze customer reviews and extract insights using AI tools to support segmentation, persona development, and strategic decisions.

AI-Driven Content Creation

Students develop brand voice and generate marketing content across channels using AI, evaluating outputs for effectiveness and alignment.

Social Media & Campaign Planning

Students build AI-powered content calendars and platform-specific strategies for social media marketing campaigns.

AI-Enhanced Advertising

Students generate, compare, and refine ad variations using AI tools, evaluating creative outputs against marketing objectives.

Customer Experience & AI Chatbots

Students design chatbot and AI-supported customer journeys, mapping touchpoints across the full customer experience.

Personalization & Recommendations

Students design personalized marketing strategies using AI, exploring recommendation systems and tailored customer communication.

Experimentation & Optimization

Students design A/B experiments and optimize AI-generated content, learning how to test and improve marketing performance systematically.

Pedagogy

Applied Learning Approach

This course is highly hands-on and application-focused. Each class session includes short conceptual lectures, live demonstrations of AI tools, in-class exercises where students actively use AI, and group-based activities simulating real marketing tasks.

Students regularly create, test, and refine marketing outputs using AI tools. Many activities are designed to mirror how marketing teams use AI in practice. A semester-long group project allows students to apply these skills in a realistic business context, developing a complete AI-powered marketing strategy from customer segmentation through to testing and final presentation.
