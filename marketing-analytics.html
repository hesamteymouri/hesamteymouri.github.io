Marketing Analytics

Data-driven marketing decision making using analytical tools, programming, and real-world datasets.

Course Description

This course introduces students to marketing analytics as a data-driven approach to understanding customers, evaluating marketing performance, and improving strategic decision making. Students learn how organizations use quantitative analysis to solve marketing problems and generate actionable insights.

The course emphasizes practical analytical skills and exposes students to modern tools used to organize, analyze, and interpret marketing data in real business contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the role of analytics in marketing decision making
  • Work with marketing data to identify patterns, trends, and customer insights
  • Apply analytical methods to evaluate marketing performance
  • Use tools such as Excel, Python, R, or other analytics platforms for marketing analysis
  • Communicate analytical findings through reports and visualizations
  • Develop data-driven recommendations for marketing strategy

What Students Learn

Marketing Data Analysis

Students work with real datasets to identify patterns in customer behavior, campaign performance, and market trends.

Data Visualization

Students learn how to present analytical findings through charts, dashboards, and clear marketing reports.

Customer Insights

Students analyze customer data to uncover behaviors, preferences, and segments that inform marketing strategy.

Programming for Analytics

Students gain exposure to tools such as Python and R for cleaning data, conducting analysis, and generating marketing insights.

Campaign Performance Evaluation

Students learn how to measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns using performance metrics and data-driven reasoning.

AI-Assisted Analysis

Students use AI-assisted tools to support data exploration, interpretation, and the generation of strategic marketing insights.

Applied Learning Approach

Students collaborate in teams on hands-on analytics projects that reflect real marketing practice. Using modern analytics platforms, programming tools, and AI-assisted analysis, they work with data to answer marketing questions, evaluate performance, and generate strategic recommendations.

These assignments emphasize practical problem solving, teamwork, and the ability to translate complex analytical results into clear and actionable marketing decisions.
