Course

Marketing Analytics

Using data, statistical analysis, and modern analytical tools to support marketing decision making.

Overview

Course Description

Businesses rely on customers to generate value and sustain growth. Marketing analytics refers to data-driven approaches that use customer information to guide decisions related to acquiring, serving, developing, and retaining customers.

However, customer data rarely provide a complete picture of the marketplace. Important elements such as customer preferences, willingness to pay, or the likelihood that a customer may discontinue using a product are often not directly observable in available datasets. In addition, datasets typically exclude potential customers who have not yet interacted with the firm. As a result, effective marketing analytics combines data with marketing theory, domain knowledge, statistical techniques, and analytical models to generate insights and support strategic business decisions.

This course emphasizes hands-on learning through data analysis and coding. Students work with real datasets and analytical tools to explore customer behavior, evaluate marketing performance, and generate data-driven insights. The course introduces programming in R and exposes students to a range of analytical techniques used in modern marketing analytics.

Goals

Course Objectives

  • Understand the role of analytics in marketing decision making
  • Analyze marketing datasets to identify patterns and customer insights
  • Apply statistical and analytical models to marketing problems
  • Use tools such as R, Python, Excel, and AI-assisted analytics tools
  • Interpret analytical results and translate them into marketing strategy
  • Communicate insights through data visualization and analytical reports
Topics

What Students Learn

Marketing Data Analysis

Students work with real transactional and behavioral datasets to explore customer behavior, clean data, and identify patterns that inform marketing decisions.

Customer Segmentation & Targeting

Students learn how firms identify distinct customer segments using clustering methods and behavioral metrics such as recency, frequency, and monetary value (RFM).

Predictive Marketing Analytics

Students build statistical models to predict outcomes such as customer churn and evaluate factors that influence customer retention.

Choice Modeling & Marketing Experiments

Students analyze how product attributes influence consumer decisions and learn how firms use A/B testing and experimental design to evaluate marketing strategies.

Customer Lifetime Value & Retention Analytics

Students estimate customer lifetime value (CLV), analyze repeat purchasing behavior, and evaluate strategies for long-term customer relationship management.

Market Basket & Retail Analytics

Students use association rule mining to identify products frequently purchased together and understand how retailers use these insights for cross-selling and recommendation systems.

Programming for Marketing Analytics

Students gain hands-on experience using R for data cleaning, statistical modeling, visualization, and applied marketing analytics.

Data Visualization & Communication

Students learn how to translate analytical results into clear visualizations and managerial insights that support data-driven marketing decisions.

AI-Assisted Analytics

Students learn how modern AI tools can support exploratory analysis, coding workflows, and insight generation in marketing analytics projects.

Pedagogy

Applied Learning Approach

Students collaborate in teams on hands-on analytics projects that simulate real marketing decision environments. Using statistical models, programming tools (R, Python), and AI-assisted analysis, they analyze marketing datasets, evaluate demand patterns, and generate strategic insights.

Assignments emphasize practical problem solving, teamwork, and the ability to translate analytical results into actionable marketing recommendations.
